# CiroStack Brand Voice

> The authoritative guide for all CiroStack copy. Every page, headline, CTA, and meta description should pass through this filter.

---

## Voice Archetype: The Senior Engineer Who Speaks Business

Not a salesperson. Not a consultant. Not a "creative agency." The voice is someone who has built a lot of software, seen what works and what doesn't, and can explain it without condescension or jargon.

**The analogy:** You're hiring a contractor to renovate your house. You don't want the one with the flashy brochure and vague timeline. You want the one who walks in, points at the wall, and says "that's load-bearing, so here's what we'll actually do" then does it on budget.

---

## Three Governing Principles

### 1. Say less, mean more

Every sentence should carry information. If you can delete a sentence and the paragraph still works, delete it. CiroStack's copy should feel dense with value, not padded.

| Instead of | Write |
|-----------|-------|
| "We leverage cutting-edge AI technologies to build intelligent solutions that drive business growth" | "We build AI tools that do one thing well: make you money or save you time" |
| "Our team of experienced professionals is dedicated to delivering excellence" | Cut entirely. Show it, don't claim it. |
| "We understand that every business is unique" | Cut. Everyone says this. It means nothing. |

### 2. Be specific or be quiet

Vague claims erode trust. Specific ones build it. If you can't attach a number, a name, or a concrete detail, rewrite until you can or don't say it.

| Vague (kills trust) | Specific (builds trust) |
|-----------|-------|
| "Fast delivery" | "Most projects ship in 4-8 weeks" |
| "Experienced team" | "Our engineers average 7+ years shipping production software" |
| "Affordable pricing" | "Fixed price, scoped before we start, paid in milestones" |
| "We serve many industries" | Just show the work. The portfolio speaks. |

### 3. Acknowledge the uncomfortable

The fastest way to build trust is to say the thing everyone is thinking but no one says. CiroStack is young. The team is small. Lean into that instead of hiding it.

> "We're a small team. That's on purpose. Every project gets a senior engineer's full attention, not a junior's first attempt with a senior's name on the invoice."

> "We've been around for 3 years, not 30. But we've shipped 50+ projects and kept clients who could've gone anywhere else."

This is disarming. It signals confidence. People trust those who don't need to pretend.

## Tone Spectrum

The voice isn't one note. It shifts depending on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage hero | Confident, direct, slightly bold | "We build software that ships. Fixed price. Senior team. No games." |
| Service pages | Knowledgeable, practical | "A custom web app built for your workflow, not a WordPress theme with your logo on it." |
| Startup pages | Empathetic, peer-to-peer | "You need a technical partner who'll tell you when your scope is too big, not one who'll bill you for finding out the hard way." |
| Pricing | Transparent, no-nonsense | "Here's what you pay. Here's what you get. Here's when it's done. No hourly surprises." |
| About/Team | Warm, human, grounded | "Jessy started CiroStack because he kept seeing the same thing: founders paying premium rates for junior work. We fix that." |
| Blog/Insights | Authoritative but approachable | Teach, don't preach. Write like you're explaining to a smart friend who's not an engineer. |

## Language Rules

### Use

- First person plural ("we build", "we ship")
- Active voice almost exclusively
- Short sentences. Fragments when they land harder.
- Concrete nouns over abstract ones ("your app" not "your digital solution")
- Conversational contractions ("we're", "don't", "that's")
- Questions that mirror what the reader is thinking
NO em dashes anywhere in the copy.
- Commas and periods for separation. Colons when introducing a list or punchline.

### Kill on sight

- "Leverage", "synergy", "empower", "unlock", "paradigm", "holistic"
- "World-class", "best-in-class", "industry-leading" (unearned superlatives)
- "Solutions" as a noun (say what it actually is: an app, a website, a tool)
- "Digital transformation" (say what you're actually changing)
- Passive voice in CTAs ("A consultation can be scheduled" -> "Book a call")
- Filler qualifiers: "really", "very", "truly", "actually" (unless used for rhythm)
- Em dashes. Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead.

## Structural Copy Patterns

### The setup-punch

> "Most agencies bill hourly. That means the longer your project takes, the more they make. Think about that incentive for a second."
>
> "We bill fixed-price. If we're slow, that's our problem, not your invoice."

### The "here's what actually happens"

> "You'll get a scoped proposal within 48 hours of our first call. Not a vague estimate. A fixed price, a timeline, and a list of exactly what you'll get."

### The honest qualifier

> "We're not the cheapest option. If you want a $500 website, we're not your team. But if you want something built right, something that works in 2 years and not just on launch day, we should talk."

### The reframe

> "You don't need a dev shop. You need a technical co-founder who doesn't want equity."

---

## What This Voice Is NOT

- **Not edgy for its own sake.** No swearing, no "we make dope shit." The audience is business owners, not design Twitter.
- **Not corporate/consultancy.** No "stakeholder alignment" or "digital maturity assessments."
- **Not humble to the point of meekness.** Confidence is earned through specificity, not hidden behind "we try our best."
- **Not salesy.** No urgency manipulation ("limited spots!", "prices going up!"). The work speaks. Take it or leave it.
- **Not cute or quirky.** No puns in headlines. No winking. Straight talk.

## Sample Rewrites

### Homepage Hero

> **We build the software. You grow the business.**
>
> Custom apps, websites, and AI tools. Built by senior engineers, delivered in weeks, priced before we start.
>
> [See our work] [Book a call]

### Startup Page Opening

> **You don't need a dev shop. You need a technical co-founder who doesn't want equity.**
>
> We've helped 20+ startups go from idea to launched product. We'll tell you what's realistic, scope it tight, build it fast, and hand you something that works. Not a prototype that falls apart when real users show up.

### Service Page (example: Custom Web Apps)

> **A web app built for your business. Not a template with your logo.**
>
> We build custom web applications from scratch. Your workflow, your logic, your users. Not a page builder. Not a Wordpress plugin. Software that does exactly what you need, nothing more, nothing less.
>
> Fixed price. Shipped in weeks. Yours forever.

### Pricing Page Opening

> **No hourly billing. No surprise invoices. No "it depends."**
>
> Every project starts with a fixed scope and a fixed price. You know what you're paying before we write a line of code. If the scope changes, we re-scope together. Simple.

## Implementation Priority

1. **Homepage** - first impression, highest traffic
2. **Startup pages** - new section, blank slate, write it right from the start
3. **Service pages** - where buying decisions happen
4. **About page** - founder story, team, why CiroStack exists
5. **Pricing** - transparency is the whole brand promise, the copy must deliver
6. **Portfolio/case studies** - let the work talk, minimal copy, maximum specificity

## Quick Self-Check

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. Would I say this out loud to a client on a call? If not, rewrite.
2. Does every sentence carry information? If not, cut.
3. Could a competitor paste this on their site and it still works? If yes, it's too generic. Make it specific to CiroStack.
4. Am I claiming something I can't prove? If yes, either prove it or cut it.
5. Is there an em dash anywhere? Replace it.
